HOW TO USE DHMB

You can get back to the main homepage at any time by tapping on “Dont*Hack*Me*Bro” at the top of the page.

Keys

Content on DHMB ranges from beginner material to enthusiast-grade. The level of every article is designated by a number of Keys, from one to three.

ONE-KEY material is the most basic. These articles introduce a topic, provide a general overview or pass along some news of broad interest.

TWO-KEY items are a bit more involved. They may elaborate upon something in more depth, get more specific or require a little more action from the reader.

THREE-KEY stuff is the most detailed. It gets deep into a topic, may be pretty narrow and will most likely suit the enthusiast.

If a subject looks interesting don’t be put off by the number of Keys. Most beginners will find Two-Key articles plenty useful, just more detailed than one Key. And One-Key news items are relevant to most everyone, beginner and enthusiast alike.

Articles

There are several types of Articles on DHMB. Use the site’s menu system to navigate among the types. You can also navigate through Articles chronilogically or based on the number of Keys.

TOPICAL articles are general presentations about a subject. They may stand alone or be part of a larger Series. Topicals are meant to be useful well beyond their publication date.

NEWS articles provide timely information about developments in security and privacy that may impact many people.

HOW-TO pieces, not surprisingly, explain how to do something. This may range from changing a single setting somewhere to a full-blown project, start to finish.

REVIEWS evaluate a product or service. These are not in any way sponsored or otherwise supported by the producer of the item under evaluation, or their competitors for that matter.

OPINION pieces feature an editorial comment on a topic or news development.

INTERVIEWS present a dialogue with a person or persons about themself; an organization, movement or profession they represent; or a topic they have an opinion about.

Tags

Every Article has at least one Tag. These help organize Articles based on their subject matter. Navigating to Articles with the same Tag(s) is a good way to find related reading and you can do so by clicking on the icon for that Tag.

Series

A SERIES is a set of related articles. These will always include links to the other items in the set. A list of all Series is also available via the menu system.

Sharing

At the end of every Article are links for sharing the content on social media sites. There are also links at the top and bottom of every page to visit the site’s social media pages.
